Bellabeat Case Study: Fitbit User Behaviour Analysis Using Python¶

Project Introduction¶

Bellabeat is a high-tech wellness company that develops smart products designed to help women monitor and improve their health. As the company seeks to expand its market presence, understanding how consumers interact with wearable fitness devices can provide valuable insights for product development and marketing strategy.

This project analyzes Fitbit fitness tracker data to explore user behaviour related to physical activity, sleep patterns, calorie expenditure, heart rate monitoring, and weight tracking. Through data preparation, feature engineering, exploratory analysis, and business-focused interpretation, the study aims to identify behavioural trends that can support data-driven decision-making.

The analysis follows a structured analytics workflow, including data cleaning, validation, relational integration, exploratory data analysis, and strategic recommendation development. The findings are intended to demonstrate how wearable device data can be transformed into actionable business insights that support customer engagement and wellness-focused product innovation.

Business Task¶

Analyze smart device usage data to identify behavioural trends and generate actionable recommendations that can inform Bellabeat's marketing strategy and product development initiatives.

Project Workflow¶

  1. Data Preparation
  2. Feature Engineering
  3. Data Modeling
  4. Exploratory Data Analysis
  5. Business Insights & Recommendations

STRUCTURAL PROFILING SUMMARY¶

A comprehensive structural profiling assessment was conducted across all six Fitbit datasets to evaluate data integrity, schema consistency, completeness, and analytical readiness prior to preprocessing and exploratory analysis. The profiling stage focused on understanding dataset granularity, variable structure, datatype validity, missing value distribution, duplication risk, and computational characteristics. The analytical environment consists of six relational datasets representing different dimensions of user wellness behaviour:

  1. Daily_activity
  2. Sleep
  3. Hourly_steps
  4. Hourly_calories
  5. Heart_rate
  6. Weight_log

These datasets collectively capture user activity, sleep behaviour, calorie expenditure, heart rate monitoring, and weight tracking across multiple temporal resolutions, including daily, hourly, and second-level granularity. Structural inspection revealed that the datasets are generally well-organized and analytically coherent. Most variables were successfully interpreted using appropriate numerical datatypes (int64 and float64), particularly for activity metrics, physiological measurements, and distance-related variables. However, temporal attributes such as ActivityDate, SleepDay, ActivityHour, and Time were initially stored as string (object) datatypes rather than native datetime formats. This creates limitations for temporal aggregation, time-series analysis, and feature engineering, necessitating datetime normalization during preprocessing. Data quality assessment showed relatively strong structural integrity across the datasets. Most tables exhibited low or negligible missing-value distributions, particularly within the core behavioural datasets (daily_activity, hourly_steps, and hourly_calories). Duplicate auditing similarly indicated minimal row-level duplication, suggesting that the exported Fitbit records maintain acceptable transactional consistency.

The datasets also exhibit varying levels of granularity and analytical complexity. Daily-level datasets (Daily_activity and sleep) provide aggregated behavioural summaries suitable for high-level wellness analysis and user segmentation. Hourly datasets (hourly_steps and hourly_calories) introduce finer temporal resolution for activity-pattern analysis and intraday behavioural profiling. In contrast, the heart_rate dataset operates at second-level granularity, representing a high-frequency physiological time-series structure requiring aggregation and optimization prior to integration with lower-granularity tables. The weight_log dataset functions as an event-based tracking table with comparatively sparse observations. Memory profiling indicated that most datasets are computationally lightweight and suitable for in-memory processing. However, the second-level heart-rate dataset demonstrates substantially higher memory consumption relative to the remaining tables, highlighting the need for careful aggregation and datatype optimization during preprocessing. Overall, the structural profiling stage confirms that the Fitbit data ecosystem possesses strong analytical potential with relatively high structural consistency and low integrity risk. Nevertheless, preprocessing operations remain necessary to standardize temporal datatypes, normalize schema conventions, validate numerical integrity, and align dataset granularity prior to relational merging and downstream exploratory analysis.

Granularity Assessment Summary¶

The Fitbit data ecosystem contains multiple temporal resolutions, ranging from daily aggregated activity records to second-level physiological observations.

The daily_activity and sleep datasets operate at a user-day granularity and are therefore suitable for direct integration. The hourly_steps and hourly_calories datasets share a common user-hour granularity, making them appropriate for hourly behavioral analysis.

The heart_rate dataset operates at a much finer user-second granularity and requires aggregation prior to relational integration to avoid row inflation and duplication. The weight_log dataset represents event-based observations and should be analyzed independently unless a specific use case requires integration.

Based on the granularity assessment, daily-level and hourly-level datasets will be merged separately to preserve analytical integrity.

STEP 6.1 - EXECUTIVE SUMMARY¶

This analysis examined user activity, sleep behavior, calorie expenditure, heart rate observations, and weight records from Fitbit smart device users.

The objective was to identify behavioral patterns that could support data-driven recommendations for Bellabeat's wellness products and marketing strategies.

Following data cleaning, feature engineering, relational integration, and exploratory analysis, several meaningful trends emerged regarding physical activity, sleep habits, and user engagement.

STEP 6.2 - KEY FINDINGS¶

1. Significant Variation in Physical Activity¶

Daily step counts varied substantially across users, indicating the presence of distinct activity segments ranging from sedentary to highly active individuals.

2. Strong Relationship Between Steps and Calories Burned¶

Users with higher step counts consistently demonstrated higher calorie expenditure, reinforcing the effectiveness of movement-based activity tracking.

3. Sleep Patterns Vary Across Users¶

Sleep duration and sleep efficiency varied considerably among participants, suggesting opportunities for personalized sleep coaching and monitoring.

4. Behavioral Differences Across Days of the Week¶

Activity levels showed noticeable variation across weekdays and weekends, indicating that user routines influence wellness behaviors.

5. Majority of Users Are Not Consistently Highly Active¶

Activity segmentation revealed that many users fall within sedentary or moderately active categories, highlighting potential opportunities for behavior-change interventions.

STEP 6.3 - BUSINESS RECOMMENDATIONS¶

Recommendation 1: Promote Activity-Based Goal Setting¶

Bellabeat should emphasize personalized step goals and daily movement targets within its mobile application. Users exhibiting lower activity levels may benefit from adaptive coaching and motivational reminders.

Recommendation 2: Expand Sleep Coaching Features¶

Given the variability observed in sleep behavior, Bellabeat can strengthen its value proposition through enhanced sleep analytics, bedtime recommendations, and recovery tracking.

Recommendation 3: Introduce Behavioral Segmentation¶

Users should be segmented according to activity levels (Sedentary, Moderately Active, Highly Active) to support personalized content, notifications, and wellness recommendations.

Recommendation 4: Leverage Weekly Behavioral Trends¶

Marketing campaigns and in-app challenges can be strategically scheduled around periods of lower engagement to encourage consistent wellness habits.

Recommendation 5: Develop Integrated Wellness Dashboards¶

Combining activity, sleep, and physiological metrics into a unified wellness score may help users better understand the relationship between daily behaviors and overall health outcomes.

Step 6.4 - Project Conclusion¶

The Fitbit dataset provides valuable insight into user wellness behaviors, particularly in relation to physical activity, sleep habits, and calorie expenditure.

The analysis revealed substantial variation in activity levels and sleep patterns, highlighting opportunities for personalized health interventions.

By leveraging these behavioral insights, Bellabeat can enhance user engagement through targeted coaching, personalized wellness recommendations, and data-driven product development strategies.

Future analysis could incorporate additional physiological indicators such as aggregated heart rate metrics and longitudinal weight tracking to further strengthen wellness modeling capabilities.

STEP 6.5 - LIMITATIONS¶

  1. The dataset represents a limited sample of Fitbit users and may not fully represent the broader wearable device market.

  2. The weight log dataset contained limited observations, restricting the depth of weight-related analysis.

  3. Heart rate data was available at a much finer temporal granularity and was not fully integrated into the daily analytical layer.

  4. The analysis focused primarily on descriptive analytics and does not establish causal relationships between variables.
