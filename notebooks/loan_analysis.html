Business Problem¶

Financial institutions face significant losses when borrowers fail to repay their loans. Identifying factors associated with loan default can help lenders improve risk assessment, reduce financial losses, and make better lending decisions.

The objective of this analysis is to explore borrower characteristics, loan attributes, and repayment behaviour to identify patterns associated with loan default risk.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset contains borrower demographic information, financial indicators, loan characteristics, and repayment outcomes.

  • Coapplicant Income
  • Loan Amount
  • Loan Term
  • Credit History
  • Education
  • Employment Status
  • Property Area

The dataset will be cleaned, transformed, and analysed to identify key drivers of loan default risk.

Key Insights¶

Key findings from the analysis include:

  • Borrowers with stronger credit histories exhibit lower default rates.
  • Loan repayment behaviour differs significantly across risk categories.
  • Income levels influence borrowing capacity and loan amounts.
  • Higher loan-to-income ratios are associated with increased risk exposure.
  • Credit history appears to be one of the strongest predictors of loan performance.

These findings can support more effective lending and risk-management decisions.

Business Recommendations¶

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed:

  1. Strengthen credit-history screening during loan approval.
  2. Monitor borrowers with high loan-to-income ratios more closely.
  3. Develop risk-based lending policies.
  4. Introduce early-warning systems for high-risk borrowers.
  5. Provide financial education programs to reduce default likelihood.
  6. Use predictive analytics to support credit-risk assessment.

These actions can improve portfolio quality and reduce default-related losses.

Conclusion¶

This project explored loan default risk using data cleaning, feature engineering, and exploratory data analysis techniques.

The analysis revealed that credit history, income levels, and risk segmentation play important roles in loan repayment behaviour. The findings demonstrate how data analytics can support better lending decisions and enhance credit-risk management.

Future work could include predictive modeling techniques such as Logistic Regression, Random Forest, or XGBoost to build a loan default prediction system.
